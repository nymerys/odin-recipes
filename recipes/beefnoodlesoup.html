Taiwanese Beef Noodle Soup

Description

Taiwanese Beef Noodle Soup is arguably the country's most recognizable signature dish. It is the national dish of Taiwan, and is featured in all areas of media, official merchandise, and can be found at every corner of the country.

Spices

The following ingredients are required to achieve the trademark balance of salty and sweet found in Taiwanese cuisine.

Soy Sauce

This is what creates the saltiness.

Rice Wine

This will bring out the fragrances of the other flavors.

Sugar

Taiwanese dishes often like to incorporate both salty and sweet flavors. Thus, sugar is usually added to Lu Rou Fan to sweeten the dish. I personally use brown sugar for health reasons, but any type of sugar can work. Rock sugar is best to generate the shiny gloss that coats the meat.

Star Anise

Star anise is perhaps the most important ingredient in Lu Rou Fan. Its potent and distinct flavor penetrates the mixture of all other spices, distinguishing the dish from other Asian cuisines. It is one of the defining flavors for Taiwan.

Bay Leaf

Bay leaves will round out the flavors.

Process

  1. Dice the beef shank into sizable chunks.

  2. Place and heat pan over medium-high heat. Add a little oil.

  3. Sautee the beef shank with a dash of soy sauce.

  4. Add soy sauce, rice wine, water, sugar, star anise, and bay leaf. Optional: Add chili oil. Ensure the beef is completely submerged.

  5. Cover and lower heat to medium-low. Let simmer for 45-50 minutes.

  6. Peel and wash carrots. Dice carrots into medium-sized chunks. Add to pot.

  7. Peel and wash potatoes. Dice potatoes into medium-sized chunks. Add to pot.

  8. Wash and dice shiitake mushrooms into fourths. Add to pot.

  9. Braise for 30-45 minutes.

  10. In a separate pot, boil water on high heat. Add noodles and boil for 10 minutes or until desired consistency.

  11. In a serving bowl, add sesame oil and white pepper. Optional: add chili paste/oil.

  12. Transfer cooked noodles from pot to bowl. Serve with beef soup. Optional: garnish with green onions.

Recipe Card

Ingredients

3-4 servings.

  • 600 grams beef shank
  • 2 carrots
  • 3-4 potatoes
  • 1/2 daikon
  • 5-6 shiitake mushrooms
  • Thick-cut flat noodles
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup rice wine
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tbsp sugar or to taste
  • 3-4 star anise
  • Optional: 2 tsp Chili oil
  • Optional: Diced green onions

Steps

  1. Dice the beef shank into sizable chunks.
  2. Place and heat pan over medium-high heat. Add a little oil.
  3. Sautee the beef shank with a dash of soy sauce.
  4. Add soy sauce, rice wine, water, sugar, star anise, and bay leaf. Optional: Add chili oil. Ensure the beef is completely submerged.
  5. Cover and lower heat to medium-low. Let simmer for 45-50 minutes.
  6. Peel and wash carrots. Dice carrots into medium-sized chunks. Add to pot.
  7. Peel and wash potatoes. Dice potatoes into medium-sized chunks. Add to pot.
  8. Wash and dice shiitake mushrooms into fourths. Add to pot.
  9. Braise for 30-45 minutes.
  10. In a separate pot, boil water on high heat. Add noodles and boil for 10 minutes or until desired consistency.
  11. In a serving bowl, add sesame oil and white pepper. Optional: add chili paste/oil.
  12. Transfer cooked noodles from pot to bowl. Serve with beef soup. Optional: garnish with green onions.
