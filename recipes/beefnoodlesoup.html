Taiwanese Beef Noodle Soup

Description

Taiwanese Beef Noodle Soup is arguably the country's most recognizable signature dish. It is the national dish of Taiwan, and is featured in all areas of media, official merchandise, and can be found at every corner of the country.

Spices

The following ingredients are required to achieve the trademark balance of salty and sweet found in Taiwanese cuisine.

Soy Sauce

This is what creates the saltiness.

Rice Wine

This will bring out the fragrances of the other flavors.

Sugar

Taiwanese dishes often like to incorporate both salty and sweet flavors. Thus, sugar is usually added to Lu Rou Fan to sweeten the dish. I personally use brown sugar for health reasons, but any type of sugar can work. Rock sugar is best to generate the shiny gloss that coats the meat.

Star Anise

Star Anise

Star anise is perhaps the most important ingredient in Lu Rou Fan. Its potent and distinct flavor penetrates the mixture of all other spices, distinguishing the dish from other Asian cuisines. It is one of the defining flavors for Taiwan.

Bay Leaf

Bay leaves will round out the flavors.

Process

Ingredients

  • 600 grams beef shank
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup rice wine
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tbsp sugar or to taste
  • 3-4 star anise
  • Optional: 2 tsp Chili oil
